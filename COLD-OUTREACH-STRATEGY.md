# Cold Outreach Strategy

Style: plain, natural, slightly imperfect, low-pressure and easy to reply to.

Core angle:
“I noticed your book is easy to find on Amazon, but there isn’t one clear page that tells Google/AI what the book is, who it’s for and where readers should go. Want me to send the quick notes?”

## Cold email
Subject: quick note on your book page

Hi [Name],

I found [Book Title] and had a quick look at how it shows up online.

The book is there, but I think Google/AI chats might struggle to clearly understand the book, genre, reader and best link to send people to.

I’m doing a few free Author AI Visibility Checks for writers.

Want me to send you the quick notes?

Cornelius
KRONATRIX Authors

## Instagram DM
Hey [Name] — found your book [Title]. Looks interesting.

Small thing: I think there may be a clearer way to show Google/AI what the book is and who it’s for.

I’m doing free quick checks for authors. Want me to send the notes?

## LinkedIn
Hi [Name], I saw your book [Title]. I work on author/book websites that make books clearer for readers, Google and AI search.

I noticed one possible visibility gap. Happy to send the quick notes if useful.

## Follow-ups
1. No worries if now isn’t the time — I can still send the short notes if useful.
2. The main thing I’d check is whether your author name, book title, genre and buy link are clear enough outside Amazon.
3. I’ll leave this here. If you ever want the quick visibility check, the link is https://bookmarketing.kronatrix.co.uk/free-author-ai-visibility-check.html

## Personalisation checklist
- Mention exact book title.
- Mention genre or cover detail.
- Mention where you found them.
- Do not over-praise.
- Ask one easy yes/no question.
