Quick answer

How to market your book

If you are wondering how to market your book, the best place to start is by making your book easy to find, easy to understand and easy to buy. A strong book marketing plan should include a clear author website, a book landing page, reader reviews, social content, email capture, Google visibility and AI search visibility.

What most authors are missing

Many authors rely on Amazon, Instagram or a link in bio. Those help, but they do not always explain the book clearly enough for readers, Google or AI chats. Your book needs one crawlable home that explains the author, book, genre, proof and next step.

Book marketing checklist

  • One clear author website
  • One dedicated page for each book
  • Cover, title, genre and reader promise
  • Buy links and email signup
  • Reviews, screenshots or proof
  • Simple HTML that Google can crawl
  • AI-readable summaries and FAQs
  • Internal links to related author pages

FAQs

Questions authors ask before building a book website

How do I market my book?

Market your book by making it easy to find, easy to understand, easy to trust and easy to buy. Start with a clear author website, one strong book page, buy links, reader proof, social content, email capture and public information that Google and AI chats can read.

How can I market my book with no audience?

Start with a small system. Build a book landing page, explain the book clearly, collect emails, post useful or entertaining content, appear on small podcasts or blogs, and send every bit of attention back to one clear page.

How do I market my novel online?

To market a novel online, make the genre, hook, characters, themes, reader promise and buy links obvious. Then create social content around scenes, tropes, quotes, worldbuilding, behind-the-scenes notes and the emotional reason readers should care.

What is the best way to advertise a novel?

The best way to advertise a novel is to send attention to a page that can convert. Ads work better when the landing page shows the cover, hook, genre, reviews, sample, author bio and clear buy links.

Do authors need a website?

Authors can promote without a website, but a website gives readers, Google, journalists, reviewers and AI chats one reliable place to understand the author, book, genre, proof and next step.

Is an Amazon author page enough?

An Amazon author page helps, but it is not enough if you want full control over your author identity, email capture, Google visibility, AI readability and reader journey. Your own website gives your book a stronger home.

How do I get my book found on Google?

Create a clean author website and book page with your author name, book title, genre, description, themes, reviews, buy links, metadata and schema. Then connect that page to social profiles, interviews, guest posts and book directories.

How do I get my book recommended by AI chats?

AI chats need clear, consistent public information. Make your author name, book title, genre, reader type, proof, website and buy links easy to read without login, heavy JavaScript or confusing layout.

What should a book landing page include?

A book landing page should include the title, author name, cover, genre, hook, description, reader promise, reviews, excerpt, buy links, author bio, FAQs, related books and structured facts AI tools can understand.

What makes an author website AI-readable?

An author website becomes AI-readable when it uses clear headings, direct answers, factual entity descriptions, schema, simple HTML, consistent names, book details and public pages that explain who the author is and what each book is about.

Yes. KRONATRIX Authors can help you build a stronger author or book page that connects to Amazon while giving Google, readers and AI chats more context than Amazon alone usually provides.
